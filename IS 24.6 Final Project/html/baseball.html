Padres Legacy Moments

  1. Historic Highlights
    • 1969: The San Diego Padres are established as an MLB expansion team.
    • 1984: Padres reach their first World Series, led by Tony Gwynn and Goose Gossage.
    • 1998: Padres win the National League pennant again, facing the Yankees in the World Series.
    • 2004: Petco Park opens in downtown San Diego — a fan-favorite ballpark with iconic skyline views.
    • 2020: The “Slam Diego” era begins as the Padres hit four grand slams in four games, a first in MLB history.
    • 2021: Joe Musgrove throws the franchise’s first-ever no-hitter against the Texas Rangers.
    • 2022: Padres defeat the rival Dodgers in the NLDS, advancing to the NLCS for the first time since 1998.
  2. Fan Favorites
    • Tony Gwynn: “Mr. Padre,” Hall of Famer, and one of the best hitters in MLB history with a career .338 average.
    • Trevor Hoffman: Legendary closer with 601 career saves and the entrance music that shook Petco — “Hells Bells.”
    • Jake Peavy: Cy Young winner in 2007 and strikeout machine.
    • Fernando Tatis Jr.: One of baseball’s brightest young stars, known for electric plays and flair.
    • Manny Machado: Defensive wizard and team leader during the Padres' recent postseason runs.
